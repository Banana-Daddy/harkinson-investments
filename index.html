The Founder · Profile

A career that started in Texas city halls and was made in the state's commercial real estate market.

Jeff Harkinson has spent 43 years putting his name — and his money — on the line.

  • BA Economics & Political Science · Austin College
  • Master of Public Administration · Southern Methodist University
  • Board · Hill Country Alliance
  • Board · Old Blanco County Courthouse Preservation Society
"
Nobody cares like an owner.
— Jeff Harkinson · founding principle, since 1983

A native Texan born in Houston and a Dallas resident since 1962, Jeff began his career in city management — ten years inside the City of Dallas City Manager's Office, the Mesquite city manager's office, and as Director of Regional Services at the North Central Texas Council of Governments, where he worked across a sixteen-county region.

In 1983, he stepped out of the public sector to form his own commercial brokerage and investment company. Eight years later, in 1991, he founded what is now Harkinson Investment Corporation, the holding firm for a portfolio that has since grown to three million square feet of office, medical, retail, and industrial space across Texas.

Between brokerage and principal investment, Mr. Harkinson has personally closed more than half a billion dollars in transactions. The portfolio carries a perfect closing record since 1991 — and across more than thirty years, has not missed a mortgage payment.

Outside the firm, Jeff is the steward of Blasingame Creek Ranch in Blanco, Texas — a Hill Country property anchored by an original 1854 log cabin, the site of the legendary shootout that killed Texas Ranger James Callahan. He sits on the boards of the Hill Country Alliance and the Old Blanco County Courthouse Preservation Society, and his interests run to environmental preservation, biodiversity, and Texas history.
